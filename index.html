2 Decision Framework — Bug Out vs. Shelter in Place

Not every disaster requires evacuation. Use this framework to decide quickly:

FactorShelter in Place ✅Bug Out 🚪
Your home is…Structurally sound, safe areaDamaged, in flood/fire zone, or in fallout path
Utilities are…Partially working or you have backupDown with no timeline for restoration
Streets are…Blocked or impassableClear enough to travel (or you go on foot)
Threat is…Temporary (storm, short outage)Escalating (unrest, contamination, long-term grid failure)
Authorities say…“Shelter in place”“Evacuate” or no comms at all
Radiation?No readings on Geiger counterRising readings — leave immediately upwind
Rule of Thumb
When in doubt and you have a clear route, it is generally better to leave early than to wait too long. Leaving 6 hours ahead of everyone else is the difference between an easy drive and gridlocked highways.

3 Scenario Quick-Reference Cards

☢️ Nuclear Event

Trigger: Flash/EBS alert, mushroom cloud visible, or Geiger counter spiking.
Action: If detonation is imminent, shelter in the most interior room (basement ideal) for first 24–48 hrs to avoid initial fallout. Then bug out on your primary route, moving upwind/crosswind from the blast site. Monitor Geiger counter continuously. Seal windows/doors with plastic and tape if sheltering. Avoid rain.
Timeframe: Shelter 24–48 hrs, then evacuate upwind.

⚡ Carrington Event / EMP

Trigger: Widespread power failure, electronics dead, no cell/internet, vehicles potentially disabled.
Action: Shelter in place initially (days 1–3) to assess scope. Fill all containers with water immediately (pumps may fail). If grid stays down past 72 hrs, prepare to bug out. Travel may require foot/bicycle. Retrieve Faraday-protected electronics from your critical electronics case.
Timeframe: Shelter 1–3 days, then evaluate.

🚨 Civil Unrest / Societal Breakdown

Trigger: Sustained rioting, supply chains halted, law enforcement overwhelmed.
Action: Keep a low profile. Avoid downtown / high-density areas. If violence approaches your neighborhood, bug out using your alternate route that avoids major population centers. Travel at night if possible. Use Meshtastic for silent encrypted text comms, maintain voice radio silence.
Timeframe: Bug out when threat reaches your area.

🍔 Food/Supply Shortage

Trigger: Grocery shelves empty 3+ days, no resupply expected.
Action: Begin rationing stored food. Shelter in place as long as water is available. Use rocket stove for cooking. Supplement with foraged edibles (research local edible plants). If shortage extends past your food reserves with no relief and water is becoming scarce, bug out toward agricultural rural areas. Barter skills and supplies.
Timeframe: Shelter as long as food and water reserves hold, then evaluate. Water is the limiting factor, not food.

🌪️ Severe Storm / Tornado

Trigger: Tornado warning, severe damage to home.
Action: Shelter in place (basement/interior room) during storm. If home is destroyed, deploy survival tents and assess. Typically short-duration — await emergency services. Bug out only if infrastructure is catastrophically damaged.
Timeframe: Shelter during event; reassess after.

❄️ Extended Power Outage (Winter)

Trigger: Ice storm or grid failure; no heat in dangerous temps.
Action: Insulate one room, use sleeping bags/thermal blankets. Use rocket stove for heat (with ventilation!). Melt snow for water if pipes freeze. Use tealight candles for supplemental light and warmth. If outage extends past 5–7 days in dangerous conditions, bug out toward your designated destination.
Timeframe: Shelter up to 1 week; bug out if worsening.

Critical Electronics Case

Smaller hard case (waterproof, wheeled recommended). Rides in the car or on top of the wagon. Use a lid organizer for documents and flat items.

  • Radiation detector / Geiger counter (padded)
  • Meshtastic relay node
  • Spare radio batteries and charging cables
  • Rechargeable batteries + USB charger holders
  • USB power tester / multimeter
  • Hand-crank radio
  • Important documents — originals in waterproof bags
  • Backup medications
  • Precision screwdriver set
⚡ Faraday Protection
Store critical electronics in Faraday bags inside your hard cases. Keep at least one handheld radio, one Meshtastic node, and your Geiger counter in individual Faraday bags at all times. Use remaining bags for: spare rechargeable batteries, relay nodes, and a phone or laptop. If an EMP kills your active devices, these protected spares become your most valuable assets.

Bulk Gear Case

Larger hard case (waterproof, wheeled recommended). Rides in the vehicle. Contains overflow and heavy items:

  • Large solar panel (folded)
  • Extra collapsible water container
  • Hand-crank radio (backup)
  • Tealight candles (portion of supply)
  • Extra food and snack bars
  • Trekking poles (collapsed)
  • Seasonal clothing, blankets, sleeping bags
  • Any firearms and ammunition
  • Duct tape, extra paracord, zip ties
  • Backup repair tools
  • Fast-charging cable
Vehicle Load Plan
Both backpacks in back seat (grab-and-go). Critical electronics case + bulk gear case in trunk/bed. Wagon on top or in bed. If you must abandon the vehicle: grab both packs + critical electronics case, load on wagon, walk. The bulk gear case is “nice to have” — leave it if you must.

8 Communications Plan

Five-tier comms system with built-in redundancy. Use the highest-capability system available and fall back as needed.

Tier 1 — Primary

Meshtastic LoRa Mesh Network (Encrypted Text + GPS)

Your most tactically valuable comms system. Encrypted text messaging and GPS position sharing over 915MHz LoRa with zero infrastructure required.

Hardware

  • Meshtastic handheld nodes — LoRa + GPS + BT + OLED display. One per person.
  • Meshtastic relay node — Deploy at rally points to extend mesh range.

Capabilities

  • Encrypted messaging: AES-256 by default. Comms are private.
  • GPS position sharing: See your team's exact location on OLED or phone app.
  • Mesh relay: Deploy relay node at rally point as repeater with store-and-forward.
  • Range: 1–5 mi urban, 10+ mi line-of-sight. Flat terrain extends range significantly.
  • Silent operation: No voice transmission for others to hear.
  • Battery life: Days of standby with periodic GPS pings.
Setup — Do This Now
  1. Flash all devices with Meshtastic firmware (meshtastic.org)
  2. Set all devices to the same encryption key (shared channel + strong PSK)
  3. Configure handheld nodes as “Client” role with GPS enabled
  4. Configure relay node as “Router” role (relay, saves battery)
  5. Set GPS broadcast interval to 15 minutes
  6. Pair each handheld node to phones via Bluetooth
  7. Test range in your neighborhood and along bugout routes
  8. Store relay node in Faraday protection in your critical electronics case

Tactical Use

  • Normal movement: All team members carry nodes powered on. GPS pings every 15 min.
  • Separation: Text your status and heading — all encrypted.
  • Rally point relay: Deploy relay node at rally point. Relays messages even when you've moved on.
  • Stealth: Voice-radio-silent in civil unrest. Meshtastic only — encrypted, inaudible.
Tier 2 — Voice

Handheld Dual-Band Radio (VHF/UHF)

4–8W output. Range: 5+ mi urban, 10–20 mi open terrain.

Pre-programmed Frequencies

  • Private simplex freq for team comms (choose an uncommon frequency)
  • NOAA Weather: [Your local NOAA freq — check weather.gov]
  • FRS Channel 1: 462.5625 MHz (to talk to backup walkies)
  • Local amateur repeaters (check repeaterbook.com for your area)
  • 146.520 MHz (national calling freq — monitor only)

Code Words

Radio discipline: Transmissions under 10 seconds. Never broadcast location, names, or plans. Prefer Meshtastic text over voice whenever possible.

Get Licensed Now
FCC Technician license: ~$15, 35 multiple-choice questions. Lets you practice legally and learn your local repeater network. In a true life-threatening emergency, FCC rules allow unlicensed transmission on any frequency.
Tier 3 — Backup Voice

FRS Walkie-Talkies

  • Simpler to use — FRS channels 1–22, no license
  • Range: 1–2 miles typical
  • Choose a primary channel and backup channel
  • Good for short-range team coordination
Tier 4 — Cell-Dependent

Smartphones & Smartwatches (LTE)

  • LTE smartwatch: calls/texts without phone if cell towers are up
  • GPS tracking regardless of cell service
  • Battery ~24 hrs — conserve for when needed
  • Not a primary comms tool — useful in partial-infrastructure situations
Tier 5 — Receive Only

Hand-Crank Emergency Radios

  • AM/FM/NOAA — zero batteries or charging needed
  • Your lifeline for official broadcasts and weather alerts
  • One per hard case recommended
  • Crank 1 minute = ~15–30 minutes listening

If All Electronics Are Dead (EMP/Carrington)

  • Retrieve Faraday-protected electronics from your critical electronics case
  • Meshtastic works without phones — handheld nodes have built-in OLED screen for messages and GPS
  • Fall back to pre-agreed rally points (Section 9)
  • Leave physical signals: bright cloth tied to a visible spot = “I was here, heading to next point”
  • Hand-crank radios should survive EMP (simple circuits) — monitor broadcasts

10 Shelter-in-Place Plan

For scenarios where staying home is the right call (short-term outage, storm, early-stage food shortage).

Immediate Actions (First 2 Hours)

  • Fill bathtub(s), all collapsible containers, and every vessel with water (water pressure may fail)
  • Charge all devices — phones, radios, Meshtastic nodes, walkies, Geiger counter, power banks — using solar panel if power is already out
  • Gather bugout bags to one staging area near the door (ready to go if situation changes)
  • Check Geiger counter if nuclear event is possible
  • Power on Meshtastic devices — monitor for messages from neighbors or other mesh users in range
  • Lock doors, close blinds, maintain a low profile
  • Tune to NOAA weather radio or AM radio for emergency broadcasts
  • Light tealight candles for room lighting if power is out (conserve battery-powered lights for mobile use)

Days 1–3: Assessment Phase

  • Ration food and water — inventory your reserves and calculate how many days they cover
  • Begin cooking bulk staples with rocket stove outdoors (ventilation!) using collected wood/debris — cook in bulk, store cooked portions
  • Establish a watch schedule (one person sleeps, one watches)
  • Monitor radio on local repeater frequencies and NOAA via crank radio for situation updates
  • Keep Meshtastic active — other mesh users in your area may relay valuable local information
  • Use power banks for overnight device charging, recharge banks via solar during the day
  • Avoid unnecessary trips outside

Days 3–7+: Extended Shelter Phase

  • If situation is stable but infrastructure is down, sustain on your food reserves
  • Supplement staples with any available pantry items, canned goods, foraged edibles (research local edible plants)
  • Manage water carefully — this is your limiting factor, not food. Purify all non-tap water with filters
  • If power inverter is available and a vehicle battery is charged, use it to run small electronics
  • Continue monitoring all comms channels for situation updates
  • Track your food reserves — begin bugout planning when you're at 50% (gives margin for travel)
Set Tripwire Conditions
Agree with your team on automatic bugout triggers: e.g., “if we hear gunfire within 1 mile, we leave” or “if water is gone and no resupply in 48 hours, we go” — don't wait until it's too late.

11 60-Second Go Checklist

When it's time to leave right now, grab these in order:

  1. Backpacks (pre-packed, staged by door) — one on each person
  2. Critical electronics case (Geiger counter, Meshtastic relay, documents, electronics)
  3. Radios — one each, confirm on your private frequency
  4. Meshtastic nodes — one each, confirm powered on with GPS fix
  5. Car keys, phones, wallets, watches on wrists
  6. Fill collapsible containers with water if time allows (60 sec at the tap)
  7. Grab any last medications from fridge/cabinet
  8. Load bulk gear case, wagon, and water into vehicle
  9. Confirm verbally: “Route _____, destination _____, rally point _____.”
  10. Lock house and go
If You Have 15–30 Minutes
Also grab: blankets/sleeping bags, extra food from pantry, fill ALL collapsible containers, pet supplies if applicable, USB drive with photos, tealight candles in a ziplock. Check Geiger counter readings before choosing your route.
